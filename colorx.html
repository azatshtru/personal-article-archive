In freshman year of highschool, I designed colorox. The puzzle game that was well-recieved by audiences enough to make me confident that the concept was good.

The prime critique was that it was too complex to understand, and that the controls and UI were a bit of the grid.

Below are the screenshots of my highschool colorox:


I held onto the idea, I've learnt a lot more about UI/UX and game design, better programming practices, shaders and everything since then.

I have developed and followed a philosophy of "simple to control, complex through the inherent properties, elementary shaped" design. Based on that, I decided to redesign colorox into color.x

color.x is a silly little puzzle game about colors and energy! where you mix colors using intelligent thinking to solve silly puzzles.

The art and gameplay follow the aesthetic of the ARC design.

Elegantly crafted levels that require you to think beyond
